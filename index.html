Bachelor of Science

Hello! This is my experience in my Bachelor degree.
I studied my Bachelor in Spain in a private university. I was always really interested in mathematics, physics and science in general. That's why I decided to study a bachelor of Engineering. I wasn't really sure what speciality to take, therefore I decided to study Industrial Engineering. I really enjoyed the bachelor degree. In fact I enjoyed it that much that I decided to continue studing for at least 3 more years.
I made my first Erasmus Program in 2017 and I came to Munich. A city that I loved. The speciality I took in Munich was automotive and logistik. I didn't learn a lot about automobiles and logistics in my Bachelor degree and I thought it was time to gain that knowledge. I enjoyed a lot programming with tools like MATLAB and electronics.
The most interesing project in my Bachelor was my Thesis. I programmed a lot in MATLAB and I really liked it. The objective of the project was to make a program for students to help them resolve tasks of a subject named "circuits of continuos and alternative currents."

First Job experience

The first job experience I had was in an automobile industry - GESTAMP - in 2017. At this time I came back from my Erasmus Program and I was finishing my bachelor. I was really motivated because I in my Eramus Program I had one really interesting subject, it was alternative enegry vehicles. This was my first experience in a multinal corporation. I thought I would like working in such an Industry but it was not for me. The company was really, really big with a lot of hierarchies, and every decision took a lot of time. I had very little responsability but I learned a lot. I learned that I like management and that Excel is frecuently used in big corporations. I learned how big projects are managed, from the beginning to the very end.
In general I think the experience was really positive and I know now that the automobile industry is not for me.

Master of Science

For my Master degree I wanted to change to a more technical university. This was the University of the Basque Country, a public univesrity and a really famous university in Spain for Engineers. I thought this was a great challenge and that I could study some more years. The Master I made is the continuation of the degree of Industrial Engineering. I decided to take the spaciality in Design and Manufacturing because I always liked designing and I consider myself a really cretive person. The Bachelors' subjects I enjoyed the most were AutoCAD and programming. I wanted to improve my knowledges in design and sustainability and took this speciality.
Some really interesting projects I made last year in the speciality in my Masters' degree was the desing of a hospital bed. This project was 1 semester long and really hard. Every mechanism of the bed had to be designed and implemented, so that the bed moved in the software.
Last year in September I started my Erasmus Program in Berlin. There I took some clases about Supply Chain Management in order to take a deeper look into companies. We had to work a lot in groups and to take a lot of presentations which I liked a lot. I think that is the way everythink works in "real" life. Not everythink is studying alone in your room or in the library. It's also really important to knoe how to talk infront of people or at least to have done it sometimes.

Siemens Experience

In my second semester in Berlin I started my Thesis in Siemens (February 2020). First I had to learn how everything works there, the "onboarding". I had to learn how every machine works, they wanted me to be really good informed of what they really do there. I appreciated that a lot and thanks to that I could learn also a lot about 3D metal printing. My supervisor wanted that I learned the most I could and showed me everything she knew. Also she let me help her with some of her projects, too.
I'm really greateful for this opportunity and for having met such kind people with whom to work so well and at ease. Also, I want to point out, I was really lucky to start before the coronavirus so that I could get to know everybody and how everything works in "normal" conditions.

Future ideas

I'm a really curious person. I always want to learn more and I always have new ideas. I have no idea where I'm going to end because I have no plans for the future. One thing I'm sure of is that I don't want to work for an automobile company. As I previously said, I like designing but also management and making calculations or programming. I work fine alone but I also enjoyed working in a group.
I want to continue learning and growing and I think this is much more easier in a start-up than in multinational corporations. That is why I look forward working in one. If there's a lot of hierarchy, proposing your own idea is very complicated and therefore, I think it's diffcult to grow both as a person and in the workplace. I think you don't learn the same thing in a multinational company as you do in a start-up, and I look forward to working in one. When there is a lot of hierarchy, proposing your own or creative ideas is very difficult and therefore I think it is difficult to grow both as a person and in the professional environment.
